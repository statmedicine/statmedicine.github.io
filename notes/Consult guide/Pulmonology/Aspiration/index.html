Aspiration

Background

Aspiration occurs when you have reduced control of swallowing function - examples of when this might happen include:

  • Stroke
  • Loss of consciousness

Approach to aspiration

  1. Airway - listen for stridor
    • If concerned about airway obstruction - consider using a suction tool to remove any visible objects
    • Place patient on their side
    • Consider abdominal thrusts and back thrusts
  2. Breathing
    • Pulse oximetry
    • Give O2 if concerned about hypoxia
    • May need to consider intubation
    • Order a chest XR for aspiration pneumonia/pneumonitis and to look for the foreign body (if you are concerned there is one)

Consider calling a rapid response if you are concerned that they will lose their airway - they will bring the airway trolley
