Diabetes consult

101

  1. Who are you
  2. Patient name, age, MRN
  3. What is the consult question
    • I'm calling because the patient is hyperglycaemic and HbA1c is high and we would like to manage his BSLs preoperatively
    • I'm calling because he is on a complex insulin regimen and we've been told hes going to be discharged home and might not manage
  4. Why are they in hospital
  5. Diabetes details
    • Type 1 or 2 when they were diagnosed
    • Management
      • Medications - eMeds or DC summary, etc.
      • GP or Endocrinologist - indication of severity
    • Control
      • HbA1c - have it ordered at least
      • Current BGLs
    • Any upcoming changes/surgery/fasting
    • Discharge destination and timing - e.g. if they go to NH, they dont need insulin teaching
    • Anything else critical (e.g. cognitive impairment, disability)

Diabetes diagnosis-related questions

  1. When were they diagnosed
  2. What type of diabetes do they have
  3. How was diagnosis made
  4. Do they experience polydipsia, polyuria, fatigue, or weight change
  5. Do they have angina, dyspnoea or palpitations
  6. Female - have they experienced gestational diabetes

Medication-related questions

  1. Insulin dependent on oral hypoglycaemic agents or diet controlled
    • How long did it take to progress to the next step of DM control?

Control-related questions

  1. Have they ever had hypoglycaemia, DNA or HHNK related complications/admissions
  2. Do they have macrovascular complications (IHD, CVD, PVD)
  3. Do they have microvascular complications (neuropathy, retinopathy, nephropathy)
    1. Do they experience erectile dysfunction
  4. Do they know their HbA1c?
  5. Do they do their blood sugars at home?
    1. How often?
    2. What values do they have? Do they have a diary?
    3. How often do they see their GP with these results?

Function-related questions

  1. How does diabetes affect their ADLs?

Follow-up related questions

  1. Do they see their GP with results?
  2. Do they have an endocrinology, cardiologist, nephrologist, optometrist, podiatrist?

Social history

  • Tobacco and alcohol consumption

Family history

  • Family history of endocrine dysfunction?

Type 1 specific questions

  1. Do they use an insulin pump?
  2. When did they transition from pediatrician to adult endocrinologist?
  3. Do they keep glucogen on them at all times?
  4. Are they storing insulin correctly?
  5. Is their glucometer working correctly?
  6. Are they rotating injection sites?
  7. Have they been screened for other autoimmune diseases?
    1. Coeliacs, Thyroid dysfunction, Addison's, Pernicious anaemia

Lab values

  1. FBC, EUC, CMP, LFT
    • EUC as poor function can contraindicate metformin
    • FBC for ?pernicious anaemia
  2. HbA1c
    • Baseline, relevant
  3. TSH
    • due to concurrent risk of autoimmune thyroiditis
  4. Fasting lipids
    • second yearly conducted due to risk of CVD, IHD, etc.
