Hypokalemia

Approach on the wards

  1. Compare to prior blood test results - perhaps longstanding, known hypokalemia?
    1. Also check for Mg+ - hypomagnesmia can make K+ supplementation difficult
  2. Assess for any symptoms
    • ECG if < 3.0mM
  3. Consider action based on K+ range
    • < 2.0mM - ICU monitoring and replacement at high rate
    • ~3-3.5mM
      • Can swallow -> Slow K (1200mg BD) or Chlorevescent (1000mg BD) - single tablets will raise serum K+ by 0.1mM
      • Cannot swallow - IV K+ via Minibags (100mL 0.9% NaCl with 10mM KCl) over 1 hour - will raise serum K+ by 0.1mM

Theory

Causes

  • GIT losses
  • Diuresis
  • Antibiotic use
  • renal impairment
  • hypomagmesaemia
  • malnutrition

Symptoms

  • Palpitations
  • constipation
  • Paraesthesia
  • Weakness
