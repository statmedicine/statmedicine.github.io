Altered mental status

Considerations

  1. In what way is the patient acutely confused
  2. Is there a decreased LOC?
  3. What are their vitals?
  4. How old is the patient
  5. Why were they admitted
  6. Have there been previous similar episodes
  7. Is there a reason for their behaviour
  8. Is the patient aggressive
  9. What measures have been attempted to reason or calm the patient
  10. Are staff or patients at risk of harm/injured?

Approach to Clinical Review

Danger

  1. Call security if patient is aggressive, confused or irrational

A - Airway

  1. Inspect airway

B - Breathing

  1. Attach Pulse oximeter
  2. Administer oxygen - make sure they don't have COPD; do not overoxygenate and risk critical hypercapnia
  3. Assess RR -
  4. Consider Gudel insertion with Bag-valve ventilation if non-responsive; may need ETT placement

C - Circulation

  1. Check HR
    • Tachycardia - associated with hypoglycaemia, hypoxia, hypotension, sepsis, alcohol withdrawal, hypothyroid
    • Bradycardia - associated with raised ICP, pre-terminal hypoxia, narcosis
  2. Check BP
    • SBP < 90mmHg - coonsider impaired cerebral perfusion; shock
    • Hypertension - ICH, drug intoxication, alcohol withdrawal
  3. 2x large bore PIVCs
  4. Send Basic Bloods

D - Disability

  1. Assess pupils
    • Constriction - ?narcosis; opioid overanalgesia (seen with bradypnoea); brainstem stroke
    • Dilation - ?critical hypoxia
  2. Assess for minor twitching (e.g. facial twitching)
    • ?non-convulsive seizure

E - Exposure

  1. Look for bruising, bleeding
    • ?coagulopathy

F - Fluids

  1. If SBP < 90mmHg, give 20mL/kg NS bolus

G - Glucose

  1. Fingerprick glucose
    • Hypoglycaemia
      • if <2.5mM - 50mL of 50% dextrose

Pharmacological de-escalation

Procedure:

  1. Prepare 5-10mg PO diazepam OR 5-10mg PO olanzapine
  2. Prepare 10mg/10mL Midazolam, or 10mg/10mL Diazepam or 10mg/10mL Haloperidol to be drawn up
